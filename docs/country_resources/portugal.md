## Cultural Considerations & Local Context

### Language
- **Primary language:** Portuguese
- Emergency services (112) have multilingual operators
- SNS 24 primarily Portuguese (limited English)
- Major tourist areas have English-speaking emergency personnel

### Regional Differences
- **Islands (A√ßores, Madeira):** Separate emergency response coordination, but same 112 number
- **Rural vs Urban:** Response times vary significantly; rural areas may have longer wait times
- **Tourist areas (Algarve, Lisbon, Porto):** Enhanced emergency services during tourist season

### Common Emergency Types
- **Forest fires (Inc√™ndios florestais):** Major risk June-September, especially in central/northern Portugal
- **Heat waves:** Increasingly common in summer (May-September)
- **Earthquakes:** Lisbon and Algarve on fault lines; last major earthquake 1969
- **Floods:** Coastal areas and river valleys at risk
- **Cardiac emergencies:** Heart disease is leading cause of death

### Cultural Practices
- Portuguese culture emphasizes community (vizinhan√ßa/bairro)
- Strong tradition of mutual aid among neighbors
- Bombeiros Volunt√°rios deeply respected community institutions
- Family-oriented culture - multi-generational households common

### Legal Protections
**Good Samaritan Protection:**
- Portuguese law generally protects those providing emergency aid in good faith
- Article 200 of Penal Code creates duty to assist in certain circumstances
- "Omiss√£o de aux√≠lio" (failure to help) can be criminal offense in some situations
- Volunteers protected when acting within their training/capability

**Key principle:** You are generally protected when helping in emergencies if you act in good faith and within your abilities.
