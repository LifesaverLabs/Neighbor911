**You don't need to wait for Neighbor 911‚Ñ¢ to be built.** The most powerful thing you can do is:

### Knock on Doors. Meet Your Neighbors. Build a Call Tree.

**Start with pencil and paper:**
1. **Walk to your neighbors' homes** and introduce yourself
2. **Ask them to join a local emergency contact network** - explain that neighbors can arrive in 2-3 minutes while ambulances take 8-15
3. **Create a WhatsApp group, NextDoor network, or simple phone list** - whatever works for your street/block
4. **Share what skills people have**: Who knows CPR? Who has an AED? Who's willing to do a wellness check?
5. **Practice helping each other** with small things first

**Recommended territory size:** 0.25-0.5 mile radius (1-2 blocks) - small enough to know everyone

### Mesh Your Network with Neighboring Networks

Once you have a local group:
- **Connect with neighboring blocks** - find other emergency networks nearby
- **Create coverage maps** - define territories so neighbors know which group covers them
- **Fill the gaps** - help neighbors in uncovered areas start their own groups
- **Build redundancy** - overlapping coverage means help is ALWAYS nearby

**The goal:** Complete neighborhood coverage where every home is within a 2-3 minute walk of someone ready to help.

### High-Incidence US Emergencies Neighbors Can Help With

Based on US data, the most common time-critical emergencies where neighbors save lives:

- **Cardiac arrest** (~350,000/year out-of-hospital) - CPR + AED within 3 minutes
- **Sexual assault / consent emergencies** (~430,000 reported/year, ~2-3 million estimated including unreported) - Rapid wellness check / doorknock, active bystander witness presence
- **Opioid overdoses** (~100,000+ deaths/year) - Naloxone delivery
- **Choking** (~5,000 deaths/year) - Heimlich maneuver
- **Falls (elderly)** (~36,000 deaths/year) - Immediate help getting up, preventing injury
- **Mental health crises** - Suicide prevention, companionship during crisis
- **Wellness checks** - Finding people who've fallen, preventing suicide
- **Addiction support** - Quit companion for nicotine/substance cravings
- **Domestic violence** - Active bystander presence, wellness checks

**Your neighborhood network can address ALL of these** - even without special equipment, just by showing up. Sometimes the most powerful intervention is simply knocking on a door or being present as a witness.

## Emergency Response Apps

### PulsePoint‚Ñ¢
**Website:** [pulsepoint.org](https://www.pulsepoint.org/)
**Status:** Operational & Deployed
**Partnership Status:** ‚ùå Not yet partnered (seeking future partnership)

**What It Does:**
PulsePoint is a proven, immediately available solution for quarter-mile (0.4 km) and half-mile (0.8 km) neighborhood CPR response. The app alerts CPR-trained citizens to nearby cardiac emergencies occurring in public spaces, helping to deliver life-saving intervention before EMS arrives.

**Coverage:**
Active in 4,500+ communities across the United States, covering millions of citizens.

**Why We Promote It:**
- **Immediate Solution:** Already operational and saving lives today
- **Proven Track Record:** Years of successful cardiac emergency responses
- **Complementary Mission:** Focuses on cardiac arrest while we expand to other emergency types
- **Future Integration Vision:** We hope to partner with PulsePoint to create a unified alert system where:
  - Neighbor 911 can send cardiac emergency alerts to the PulsePoint calmunity
  - PulsePoint alerts can dispatch our Minute Responders
  - Response coverage is maximized across both networks

**How to Get Involved:**
- Download the PulsePoint Respond app (iOS/Android)
- Ensure you are CPR certified
- Enable notifications for your area

## CPR & AED Training Organizations

Life-saving skills start with training. These organizations provide CPR, AED, and first aid certification across the United States:

### American Red Cross
**Website:** [redcross.org/take-a-class](https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class)

**Courses Offered:**
- Adult/Child/Infant CPR & AED
- First Aid/CPR/AED Combined
- Babysitting & Childcare Training
- Lifeguarding Certification
- Wilderness & Remote First Aid

**Format:** In-person, blended (online + in-person skills session), online
**Certification Duration:** 2 years
**Cost:** Varies ($40-$110 typical range)

**Why We Recommend:**
The Red Cross has been training Americans in life-saving skills for over 100 years. Courses are widely available, well-respected, and meet OSHA and other regulatory requirements.

---

### American Heart Association (AHA)
**Website:** [cpr.heart.org](https://cpr.heart.org/)

**Courses Offered:**
- Heartsaver CPR AED (lay rescuer)
- Heartsaver First Aid CPR AED
- Basic Life Support (BLS) for Healthcare Providers
- Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support (ACLS)
- Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS)

**Format:** In-person, blended learning, online skills check
**Certification Duration:** 2 years
**Cost:** Varies by location ($50-$150 typical range)

**Why We Recommend:**
The AHA is a leading authority on cardiovascular care and emergency response. Their CPR guidelines are the gold standard used by healthcare professionals worldwide.

## Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) Training

**Website:** [community.fema.gov/PreparednessCommunity/s/community-emergency-response-team](https://community.fema.gov/PreparednessCommunity/s/community-emergency-response-team)

**What It Is:**
CERT is a free national program administered by FEMA that trains civilians in basic disaster response skills including:
- Fire safety and suppression
- Light search and rescue
- Disaster medical operations
- Team organization and disaster psychology

**Format:** Usually 20+ hours of in-person training over several weeks
**Cost:** **FREE** (provided by local emergency management agencies)
**Certification Duration:** No expiration, but annual refresher training recommended

**Why We Promote It:**
- **Community Building:** CERT creates organized volunteer teams ready to assist during disasters
- **Comprehensive Training:** Goes beyond CPR to cover a wide range of emergency scenarios
- **No Cost:** Completely free training from qualified emergency management professionals
- **Local Coordination:** CERT teams work directly with local first responders

**How to Find a CERT Program:**
1. Visit the [FEMA CERT website](https://community.fema.gov/PreparednessCommunity/s/training-type?id=a09t00000005FRZAA2)
2. Contact your city or county emergency management office
3. Search "CERT training [your city/county]" online

## Stop the Bleed Training

**Website:** [stopthebleed.org](https://www.stopthebleed.org/)

**What It Is:**
A national campaign to train civilians in bleeding control techniques, including tourniquet application and wound packing.

**Why It Matters:**
Uncontrolled bleeding is a leading cause of preventable death. Stop the Bleed training empowers bystanders to save lives in trauma situations (mass casualty events, accidents, etc.).

**Format:** In-person courses (typically 1-2 hours)
**Cost:** Often free through community programs
**Find a Course:** [stopthebleed.org/training](https://www.stopthebleed.org/training/)

---

## Mental Health First Aid

**Website:** [mentalhealthfirstaid.org](https://www.mentalhealthfirstaid.org/)

**What It Is:**
An 8-hour course that teaches how to identify, understand, and respond to signs of mental illness and substance use disorders.

**Why We Promote It:**
Many Neighbor 911 emergencies involve mental health crises. Mental Health First Aid prepares responders to provide compassionate, effective support until professional help arrives.

**Cost:** Typically $25-$50 (often free through community partners)
**Format:** In-person or virtual instructor-led
